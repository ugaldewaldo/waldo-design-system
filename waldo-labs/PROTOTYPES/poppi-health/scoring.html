How Reputation, Presence, and Resonance are computed.

Every contributor below maps a raw signal to a 0–100 component, weighted into a single score. This page documents the exact equations, the /bxcapi endpoints and properties they require, and whether each one exists today.

Status legend. ✓ Available = exists in /bxcapi today · ⚠ Partial = endpoint exists but a needed property doesn't · ✗ Gap = MVP requirement, not in /bxcapi yet · ◷ V2 = deferred past MVP (no reliable source yet, or signal won't be accurate). In Reputation and Presence, V2 contributors drop from the formula and their weight redistributes proportionally to the remaining contributors. Resonance at v1 is derived directly from Reputation and Presence — see that section.

Notation used in every equation
  • μ_i, σ_i — brand's trailing-28d mean and stdev of raw signal s_i
  • Z(s) = clamp((s − μ) / σ, −3, +3)
  • Norm(s) = 50 + 16.67 · Z(s) — maps to 0–100, 50 = your own median
  • Norm⁻(s) = 50 − 16.67 · Z(s) — inverse contributors
  • decay_τ(d) = exp(−d / τ) — age decay in days
  • Σ w_i = 1 — weights sum to 1 per score
Reputation · Recovery analog

Reputation — how the world is receiving the brand, with credit for how it's handling what it can.

Two brands with identical sentiment can have different Reputation scores depending on how they respond. A negative spike that's been engaged with, acknowledged, and reframed shouldn't score the same as one that's been left to fester.

Reputation = clamp(
  0.35·Norm(NetSentiment)        + 0.20·Norm⁻(ComplaintPressure)
+ 0.10·Norm(NewsTone)             + 0.15·Norm(ResponseRate24h)
+ 0.10·Norm(ClusterEngagement)    + 0.10·Norm(VoiceConsistency),
  0, 100
)
Presence · Strain analog

Presence — how much of the category conversation the brand is generating, and whether it's quality.

The score the brand has the most control over. Raw share of voice is the trunk, but quality multipliers — cadence fit, freshness, whitespace — prevent the score from rewarding spam.

Presence = clamp(
  0.35·Norm(SOV)                + 0.15·Norm(CadenceFit)
+ 0.15·Norm(Freshness)          + 0.10·Norm(ChannelBreadth)
+ 0.10·Norm(WhitespaceHitRate)  + 0.15·Norm⁻(CategoryHeadwind),
  0, 100
)
Resonance · Synthesis

Resonance — is the brand's attention productive given its current reputation?

For v1, Resonance is derived directly from Reputation and Presence. The intuition: your attention (Presence) is only as productive as your standing (Reputation) makes it — and the formula penalizes both wasted goodwill and overexposure. Richer contributors (earned-to-owned ratio, founder voice carry, love/pain velocity, search interest) are deferred to V2 — see the V2 section below.

Base Resonance     = 2·Reputation·Presence / (Reputation + Presence)
                     (or 0 if both are 0)

Target Presence    = 25 + 0.75·Reputation

Overexposure       = max(0, Presence − Target Presence)
Underactivation    = max(0, Target Presence − Presence)

Over Penalty Wt    = 0.20 + 0.40·(1 − Reputation/100)
Under Penalty Wt   = 0.05 + 0.15·(Reputation/100)

Resonance = clamp(
    Base Resonance
  − Overexposure   · Over Penalty Wt
  − Underactivation · Under Penalty Wt,
  0, 100
)
Why a harmonic mean

The base score uses the harmonic mean of Reputation and Presence — not the arithmetic average. Harmonic means are pulled toward the smaller of the two values, so a brand with one strong dimension and one weak one is graded by the weak one, not the average.

Example: Rep = 100, P = 20 gives Base = 33.3, not 60. You have one thing going for you, but it's not being used.

Why a Target Presence curve

A brand with higher reputation has earned more permission to show up. Target Presence is the proportional share of category attention that fits your standing: 25 + 0.75·Reputation.

Presence is already category-relative (your share within your competitive set), so the same curve works for a multinational, a regional chain, and a single-location service. No profile picker needed.

Why asymmetric penalties

The overexposure weight grows as Reputation falls — being loud when you're disliked is the worst sin (0.20 → 0.60 as Reputation drops from 100 to 0).

The underactivation weight grows as Reputation rises — wasted goodwill is more costly when you have more of it (0.05 → 0.20 as Reputation rises from 0 to 100). Quiet rebuilding gets a soft penalty; sitting on a strong brand gets a sharper one.

Why no compliance dial

I originally proposed a separate compliance_sensitivity for regulated industries (therapy, finance, legal) so they'd be penalized harder for overexposure. Dropped it: regulated brands that overexpose accumulate negative mentions and complaints → Reputation drops → the overexposure weight climbs naturally via the existing formula.

The system self-corrects without a separate categorical dial that would have broken at the edges (multi-location services, multinationals).

Tier C · long-tail brands · ◷ V2

Small-brand contributors

For brands under ~50 mentions/month (a local therapist, solo creator, single-location service), the default Reputation contributors will eventually get gated off and these three take their place. Deferred to V2 — the underlying review and local-search endpoints don't exist yet. For v1, small brands run on the standard methodology with the universal Resonance formula (which works across brand sizes because Presence is already category-relative).
